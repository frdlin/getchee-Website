Support Philosophy
The getchee support philosophy centers on careful planning, installation, and training. Given that those steps are taken, it is believed that support requirements will be at a minimum. In principle, if the software or data supplied fails, then it is our responsibility to correct it at our cost. If there is a change of use, a change in environment, or a change in user, then resolution of issues associated with these changes would be chargeable to the customer. The following provides more detail to support this approach.

Support Backbone
We put a strong emphasis on consultancy and training to ensure effectiveness of the selected solution. The same team of experienced staff that supports customers during the point of sale continues as the customer support team upon purchase. This approach ensures that the sound understanding of the customer's business objectives, requirements, and processes is carried forward and applied throughout the support relationship.

We retain copies of all customer systems and data, such that we can replicate the customers view as accurately as possible. As such, we are in a position to resolve a range of support queries from basic user knowledge to solving business based analysis problems and simple technical support.

Support Service Level

The support service is available 08:30 to 18:30 (GMT +8) Monday to Friday, excluding national holidays. For high priority issues, please call (886) 2 7722-3600 between 08:30 and 18:30 (Monday through Friday).

Security & Password Policies

Great Passwords
We take security very seriously, and because so, we have some password policies in place:

  • Username must be a real email address. (not case sensitive)
  • Password must be at least 8 characters.
  • Password must be a combination of letters and numbers. (case sensitive)
  • Username and password cannot be the same.
  • System will require a new password every 4 months.
  • Old password and new password cannot be the same.

Secure Socket Layer (SSL)
This is the standard security technology for establishing an encrypted link between a web server and a browser. This link ensures that all data passed between the web server and browsers remain private and integral. SSL is an industry standard and is used by millions of websites in the protection of their online transactions with their customers.

When your browser connects to an SSL server, it automatically asks the server for a digital Certificate of Authority (CA). This digital certificate positively authenticates the server's identity to ensure you will not be sending sensitive data to a hacker or imposter site. The browser also makes sure the domain name matches the name on the CA, and that the CA has been generated by a trusted authority and bears a valid digital signature.

Create Study Site

  • Play Video
  • Download Video

Create a Study Site to gather data on the surrounding environment and compare with analog sites.

  1. Open the Add Study Site tool in the Add Location module.
  2. Click the map to add geographic coordinate.
  3. Fill in Basic Information and click Save.
    1. Please be patient and do not click twice because two entries will be created. A dialog box will appear when a site is successfully added.
    2. Note that Six Segments and Segment Name must be filled in to attach an ML Scorecard to the site. In addition, Branches do not have ML Scorecards.
  4. To view the newly added site, open the Locations Manager Panel.
  5. Select the Study Sites filter and find the newly created site.
  6. Click info on the far right to zoom to the site and view detailed information such as the Surrounding Environment (SE) score and analog comparison sites.

Add/Edit Micro-Location (ML) Scorecard

  • Play Video
  • Download Video

Assess on-site factors and input them into the ML Scorecard to combine a macro and micro assessment of the Study Site.

  1. Open the Add ML Score tool in the Score Card module.
  2. Select Study Sites as site type.
  3. Select the Study Site from the dropdown menu and click Send.
  4. Make a site visit, fill in the ML Scorecard and click Save.
  5. To view the ML score, open the Location Manager Panel.
  6. Select the Study Sites filter and find the site with the new ML score.
  7. Click info on the far right to open the Details Panel.
  8. View the ML score in the Location Information box.
  9. To edit the ML Scorecard, click Edit ML Score in the Details Panel.

Move/Relocate ATMs

  • Play Video
  • Download Video

Follow this procedure to move or relocate an ATM to a new geographic location.

  1. Select a province from Map Extent.
  2. Open the Location Manager and select the Stores filter to show Current Stores.
  3. Click info on the far right to open the Details Panel and zoom to the Store.
  4. Select Edit Current Store.
  5. Hover the cursor over the Store and the cursor should change into a multi-directional cursor. Zoom in closer if it doesn’t appear.
  6. Click and drag the site to a new location.
  7. Edit the Basic Information as needed and click Save.

Close Current Store

  • Play Video
  • Download Video

Change the status of a Current Store (ATM) to Closed, indicating that the ATM has been removed.

  1. Select a province from Map Extent.
  2. Open the Location Manager and select the Stores filter to show Current Stores.
  3. Click info on the far right to open the Details Panel and zoom to the Store.
  4. Click Edit Current Store.
  5. Select “Closed” from the Store Status dropdown menu and click Save.
    1. Please be patient while the site status updates. A dialog box will appear when the change is successful.
  6. To view Closed Stores, first open the Data Layers panel. Then open the Krungsri ATM layer group and turn on the Closed ATM layer.

Batch Upload Transaction Data

  • Play Video
  • Download Video

Batch upload transaction data to update ATMs using a template.

  1. Open the Batch Upload tool in the Performance module.
  2. Click Template at the bottom to download an Excel template.
  3. Fill in the data.
    1. Note that the file name and column headers should not be changed so the system can interpret the template.
    2. All transaction entries should be numerical without commas.
    3. The example entry can be deleted.
  4. Return to Kiwi and click Browse and navigate to the filled in template.
  5. Once Kiwi verifies the data, an Upload button will appear. Click Upload to complete the process.
    1. Please be patient while data is being uploaded. A dialog box will appear when the data is successfully uploaded.
